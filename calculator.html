  • % w/v — most common in analytical chemistry (e.g. 0.9% NaCl saline)

  • % v/v — used for liquid/liquid mixtures (e.g. 70% ethanol)

Notes on unit conversion

  • Molar mass is required to convert between molar (mol/L) and mass-based (g/L, mg/dL) units.
  • 1 mg/L ≈ 1 ppm by mass for dilute aqueous solutions (density ≈ 1 g/mL).
  • g/dL is commonly used in clinical labs (e.g. blood glucose, haemoglobin).

Choosing the right buffer

  • Pick a buffer whose pKₐ is within 1 pH unit of your target pH — this is the buffering range where it works best.
  • For biological systems: use HEPES or MOPS; avoid phosphate when working with Ca²⁺ or Mg²⁺.
  • For Tris buffers: always adjust pH at the temperature you will use the buffer — not at room temp then in the cold room.
  • Zwitterionic buffers (MES, PIPES, HEPES, MOPS) generally have less interference with enzyme assays and metal chelation.
