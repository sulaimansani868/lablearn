About me

I am a laboratory scientist with over ten years of experience working in university analytical and biochemical laboratories. My work has spanned quantitative and qualitative chemical analysis, biochemical assay development, method validation, and the operation and maintenance of a wide range of analytical instrumentation.

Over the years I noticed that students and junior researchers often struggled with the same things — calculating reagent concentrations, interpreting instrument outputs, preparing standard solutions correctly, and navigating safety requirements. The answers were always out there, but scattered across expensive textbooks, dense journal articles, and inconsistent online sources.

I built LabLearn to change that. Every tutorial, calculator, and app on this site is built from real bench experience — the kind of knowledge that gets passed down informally between colleagues, but that every laboratory user deserves access to from day one.

Alongside laboratory work, I have developed skills in mobile application development and web technologies, which is what made it possible to turn years of accumulated knowledge into software tools that students and researchers can carry in their pockets.

Why this site exists

Four principles behind LabLearn

🔓

Free forever

Good laboratory education should not cost money. Every tutorial, calculator, and app will always be free — no paywalls, no subscriptions, no registration.

🔬

Experience-first

Everything on this site comes from real laboratory work. Not reformatted textbook content — practical knowledge from ten years at the bench.

📐

Precision matters

Laboratory errors have consequences. Every formula, procedure, and calculation tool is checked carefully and written to the standard expected in professional analytical work.

🌍

Accessible everywhere

All apps work offline. All tools run in the browser. No account, no internet connection, no institutional access required — whether you are in a university lab or a rural health centre.
